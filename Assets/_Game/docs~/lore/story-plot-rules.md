# Story Rules & Plot Architecture

To ensure narrative consistency and prevent "plot drift," the following rules and beats must be followed by all contributors.

## The 10 Commandments of Maou-Sama
1.  **Authority, Not Cruelty:** The Maou rules because of a natural hierarchy, not necessarily because they enjoy suffering.
2.  **The Pact is Absolute:** The "World" (Gaia) can force the Maou's body to move against their will if the Hero's life is at risk (Act 3).
3.  **Low-Tech, High-Will:** Magic exists, but it feels heavy, ancient, and costly. No "light shows."
4.  **The Church is the Mirror:** The Church of Heroes is just as corrupt and power-hungry as any demon, but they hide it under "Prophecy."
5.  **Ignis is the Soul:** Ignis is the bridge between humanity and the Maou. Her loyalty defines the player's motivation early on.
6.  **The Hero is the Foil:** Arthur is a clone (fake); the Rival is the real deal. The Rival must be genuinely powerful and intimidating.
7.  **Gaia is the Shadow Protagonist:** The World is actively playing both sides. It wants the First Maou stopped, and you are its chosen antibodies.
8.  **Eldritch Undercurrent:** The "Inverse World" is a source of cosmic horror (Eyes, tentacles, shattering reality).
9.  **Bittersweet Progress:** Every victory for the Maou is a loss for their personal freedom (due to the Pact).
10. **The Final Rematch:** The ultimate goal is the rematch with the Rival, but the path there is blocked by the World's interference.

## Main Plot Anchors (The North Star)
*   **Act 1 Awakening:** Reclaiming the Citadel. Establishing that the world has forgotten the Maou, but Ignis hasn't.
*   **Act 2 Escalation:** The clash with the Church. Defeating the "Fake Hero" Arthur.
*   **Act 3 Revelation:** The sky shatters. The Inverse World invades. The "Unwilling Alliance" with the Rival.
*   **The Climax:** Defending the Rival while they charge the God-Slayer. The realization that Gaia is the master.

## Avoidance (Don't Drift Here)
*   **No Typical "Saving the World":** You are saving the world because YOU want to rule it/kill the rival, not for altruism.
*   **No Harem Tropes:** Vassals are soldiers and companions, not romantic trophies.
*   **No Power-Scaling Bloat:** Power should come from "Sovereign Authority" and strategy, not just "Getting a higher level."
